# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

First-time and returning Wing Express customers in and around Little Rock, Arkansas. Most will visit on a phone while deciding what to eat, checking current hours and availability, or placing a pickup order during service hours.

## Product Purpose

Tell Wing Express's home-grown story and convert interest into confirmed pickup orders. The site should make the full menu understandable, let customers configure wings without friction, support online payment or pay at pickup, and give the business a practical foundation for future Stripe, notification, and POS connections.

## Brand Personality

Home-grown, bold, unique, community-driven, and serious about great food. The voice is direct, welcoming, energetic, and unmistakably local.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic national-chain restaurant templates, overly corporate polish, sterile luxury styling, and complicated ordering flows. The site should not feel like a venture-backed delivery app or a theme with the logo pasted onto it.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with appetite and story, then make ordering the obvious next action.
2. Keep the menu fast to scan and easy to configure on a phone.
3. Preserve the existing red, black, and white identity while giving it a distinctive digital expression.
4. Communicate operational truth clearly: pickup only, current hours, preparation timing, sold-out items, and confirmation status.
5. Build real connection points for payments, notifications, and POS integration instead of visual-only controls.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Maintain strong contrast, visible keyboard focus, semantic controls, touch targets of at least 44 pixels, reduced-motion support, screen-reader status announcements, and ordering flows that do not depend on color alone.
